PROJECTS



Zig-Zaggy Lives
This animation visualizes the path a cohort of students takes over the course of ten years.

It aims to identify patterns in when and why an institution may struggle with retention, and helps bring attention to critical points in time.
Majors Over Time
This simple animation demonstrates changes in popular career paths by tracking the shifts of top majors at a university over the course of a decade.
Double Majors
Using a basic chord diagram, this example depicts double-majors we commonly see grouped together, using unique student/major combinations seen within a three-year time span.

Nodes and arcs are colored by the home college in which those primary majors reside.
Employment Outcomes
Public universities like to understand how many of their graduates stay within the state and give back to the local economy. Oftentimes this has a real impact on the levels of state funding they receive.

This sankey uses U.S. Census Bureau data to show what proportion of graduates from Colorado's 14 public 4-year institutions stay within the region.
Demographic Shifts
This proportion plot shows the changing demographics of students who are pursuing an engineering degree. Some metrics have seen greater shifts (e.g. race/ethnicity) than others (e.g. sex) within the past ten years. This plot helps to identify points of growth, and points of celebration.
Formatting
Previously, colleagues of mine spent an inordinate amount of time cleaning and formatting data to match very defined standards for state and federal data uploads. This Shiny app is a simple example of an automation script that elimated that process.
Predictive Modeling
This project uses an ensemble model to predict whether a particular group of students will be retained a year later. This helps leadership be proactive about outreach and intervention efforts.
Retention and Graduation
Retention and graduation rates are metrics that public institutions are required to make public in Colorado. Historically, this had been done by posting PDFs, but they were often 'walls of text' that were difficult to absorb, and difficult to utilize in strategic decision-making. Converting these data into useful, useable dashboards not only made the information easier to digest, but automated the process.
